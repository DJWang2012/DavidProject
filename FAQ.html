"If you don't think you look stupid, you can't go wrong."

What is your favorite song of all time?

A: It’s probably a tie between Scheherazade by Rimsky-Korsakov, or Bad by Michael Jackson.

Wow! That’s a big leap in genre and style. Do you just listen to everything?

A: Well…yes. Actually, I only really started listening to songs written after 1950 during my junior year of high school. Before that, I was a pure orchestra geek. I’m still an orchestra geek, but I just don’t have time for orchestra ?.

How does this all reflect your interest in math/cs?

A: They say computer science is math in disguise, but then I’d say the same about music. At the end of day, both are about a search for patterns that bring answers, in one case, to how the universe may work, and the other, what pleases and stimulates are deepest emotions and desires. We are counting and evaluating in everything we do, and music and cs are no different.

That’s deep. What made you pursue computer science at UIUC?

A: I was going to be math major, but changed my mind literally the day I sent in the application. Yes, the job prospects are good, but I’d like to think it is because I find greater joy in seeing my reasoning work itself out in a tangible way.

Why did the chicken cross the street?

Long Answer: That would be a question of qualia. Since it is impossible to understand what consciousness means for anything other than ourself, nor communicate with 100% accuracy such matters anyways, I cannot be qualified to comment on the intent of the said chicken. Short Answer: I don't know. I'll ask Professor Zilles.
